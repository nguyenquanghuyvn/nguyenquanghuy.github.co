Experimental · Environmental · Agricultural Economics

Research on information, incentives, and sustainable decisions.

I am an economist with a PhD from the University of Strasbourg. My research studies how information, incentives, and institutions shape sustainable technology adoption, food choices, and climate adaptation.

I combine laboratory and field experiments, applied econometrics, evidence synthesis, and economic theory. Across these approaches, I focus on a common question: how can policies and contracts help people make better decisions when information is incomplete, risks are unevenly distributed, and incentives are imperfect?

Currently seeking postdoctoral and research opportunities.
