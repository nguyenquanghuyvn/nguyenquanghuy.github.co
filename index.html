About

An economist working across research and applied data analysis.

My research examines how information, social influence, risk, and institutional design shape individual choices. I have designed and implemented laboratory and field experiments, developed experimental software, managed survey and experimental datasets, and communicated findings through academic papers and international conferences.

Alongside my economics training, I use Python, R, SQL, and machine-learning tools for data preparation, statistical modelling, visualisation, and reproducible analysis. I am particularly interested in research positions where rigorous economic reasoning and modern data methods can be combined.
