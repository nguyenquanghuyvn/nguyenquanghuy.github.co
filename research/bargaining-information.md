## Motivation

Farmers often obtain information about new agricultural technologies
from other farmers. However, informed farmers may have limited
incentives to communicate valuable private information.

This paper examines whether bargaining over the economic surplus
created by information can encourage information sharing.

## Research question

Can bargaining provide incentives for informed farmers to share
decision-relevant information with less-informed farmers?

## Theoretical framework

We develop a bargaining-over-information-surplus model in which a
leader possesses private information about the expected returns to
organic agriculture.

The leader and follower may negotiate how to divide the surplus
generated by credible information sharing.

## Experimental design

We conducted a lab-in-the-field experiment with farmers in Northern
Vietnam.

The experiment measures:

- Willingness to enter bargaining
- Information-sharing decisions
- Organic land-allocation decisions
- Bargaining outcomes
- Individual risk preferences
